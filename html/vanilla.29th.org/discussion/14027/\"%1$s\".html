Honorable Discharge (Carl D. Bergstrom)

T4 Phariss [Ret.]
September 2016 in Discharges

HONORABLE DISCHARGE

FROM THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

This is to certify that

Carl D. Bergstrom     2Lt.     CP1 HQ

was Honorably Discharged from the

UNITED STATES ARMY

on the 8th day of September 2016. This certificate is awarded

as a testimonial of Honest and Faithful Service





ARPC-OPM-O

DATE: 08 SEP 2016

MEMORANDUM FOR Commander,

U.S. Army 29th Infantry Division, ATTN: AHRC-OP-S, 1 Reserve Way

Fort Belvoir, VA 22060


SUBJECT: Unqualified Resignation


1.I hereby tender my unqualified resignation as an Enlisted Soldier in the

United States Army under the provisions of Chapter 6, Section III,

AR 135-175.

2.I understand that if my resignation is accepted I am entitled to an

honorable separation and will be furnished an Honorable Discharge

Certificate. I understand that I cannot resign if I am a statutory obligor.


Carl D. Bergstrom
Signature
Carl D. Bergstrom
Name (Type or Print)
Private Rifleman Charlie Company, First Platoon Headquarters
Rank Position Platoon/Squad

It is with a heavy heart I must tender my resignation from the 29th. The real world, something we all struggle to accommodate in our 29th careers, has upset the delicate balance I had managed for 5 ½ years.
I joined this unit soon after moving to a new city, not knowing anyone and going through a breakup. I needed an outlet for fun and socialization and I found that here among like minded people. For those veterans of many years and for those who are relatively brand new, you should know that you are among good friends and good people. The time and effort you put into your 29th career pays dividends both for you and for the many others who make this unit their online home.
The magic of the 29th I believe lies in its structure, be it in game or in our version of reality, there is a form and a weight of history that keeps us all tied together, enables us to climb any obstacle. It may take us a bit, but we get there and that is part of what makes it such an enduring group.
The 29th existed before I joined and it shall exist long after this speech ends.
To the SLs and ASLs, FTLs and aspiring leaders of CP1: you are now or will be part of a grand tradition of excellence in leadership which CP1 has worked hard at since the dawn of the 29th. You are carrying on a tradition and the weight of that should be on your shoulders, yet not weigh you down. You are leaders, chosen for abilities and traits which set you apart from others. Your job is to inspire, to direct, and to observe and report. You chart the life of every member in your squad, learn their quirks and put them to good use. I Salute you for you are the motor which keeps this big beautiful beast moving.
As a PFC I was asked in a unit wide survey what my aspirations were. I wanted to lead a Platoon and become an officer. I have succeeded in both. Were I to be asked tha question today, I would say - I want my Platoon to grow, to be the best ARMA unit on the block and a pillar of the community. My 29th aspirations are no longer about myself, they are about you. And you are well on your way CP1.
I am proud to have got to know all of you in one way or another. Though I may be gone from the parade ground, you all will never - ever - be gone from my thoughts. I will bleed blue and grey until the day I die.
29th, Charlie Company and CP1 - this will not be my last salute, not by a long shot, but it will do for now.
Salute
Reason for leaving
