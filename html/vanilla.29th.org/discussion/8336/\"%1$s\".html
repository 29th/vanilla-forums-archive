Promotion (Daniel L. Littlefield)

MSgt. Robles
December 2015 edited December 2015 in Promotions and Awards

2Lt. Pinckney said:

I have had the privilege of knowing Cpl. Littlefield for quite some time. We have played several different games together as well as hung out on TS quite often. I've seen him grow not only in his leadership abilities but also as a person. When I was in AP3, I knew that I could trust him to handle anything that I passed down. He never complained or acted in a negative manner and I just wish it could have been myself who promoted him to Sgt. Congrats, Sgt. Littlefield.


Sgt. Lamb said:

Cpl. Littlefield made the step up from ASL to SL back in the summer and has taken to it like a duck to a pond. Since taking over, his squad have improved in every quantifiable metric from attendance to recruiting, have been putting in some great combat performances and (most importantly) seem to be having a huge amount of fun doing this all. That being said, SSgt. Chevarie, Co. HQ and myself are all in agreement that it is high time we stopped referring to him as Cpl. Littlefield and instead promote him to Sergeant.Congratulations Sgt. Littlefield!

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Daniel L. Littlefield     76561198015593279     AP3S1

I do promote him to Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 7th day of DECEMBER 2015.

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
