Six Years of Service (Kit G. Lamb)

MSgt. Robles
November 2015 in Promotions and Awards

2Lt. Pinckney said:

Sgt. Lamb has been in the 29th for as long as I can remember. I can't help but to think about Sgt. Lamb when I think about the 29th and vise versa. He is an outstanding leading, an example for his platoon to follow as well as one of the people in the 29th that I consider to be a friend. We have served together in various places including AP2, AP3 as well as BP1 way back when we were both Pvts.

SSgt. Chevarie said:

Sgt. Lamb has been with me in Platoon HQ for now a good 3 months and it already feel like a long time. He is SO pro-active and on top of everything going on in the platoon that it looks like he's been a platoon Sgt all his life. Still, the rather short time he's been with us in Platoon HQ and even in AP3 as a whole is not entirely representative of how Hardworking he is because Sgt. Lamb has a LOT more under his belt time-wise in the 29th. He just reached a milestone that very few people have and will ever meet in the whole history of the 29th. Sgt. Lamb has dedicated 6 Years to the 29th. It's a damn lot !

6 Years is 313 weeks with +/- 3 drills a week, it sums up to 939 Drills that Sgt. lamb was on the roster for. 6 Years in one life dedicated to a game/gaming unit is a terrific achievement (only matched by guys like this)

So here it is ! I'll stop my rambling about how long 6 years and let you think about it.

In the meantime, it's my pleasure to Award Sgt. Lamb his 12th AOCC and his 3rd WW1 Victory Medal.
Love you!
