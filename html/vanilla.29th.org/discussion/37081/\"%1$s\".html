Promotion (Aristidi L. Deb)

T5 Murray
April 2020 edited April 2020 in Promotions and Awards

2Lt. Brenholen said:

I have long seen the day that the now Sgt. Deb would get this promotion. I've had the pleasure of knowing the Sgt from the start of his 29th Career and saw his potential early on, I just wanted to see him earn it. I noticed his communication and game play skills would be a great fit for the 29th. Little did I know he would become one of the most hardworking and dedicated members I have ever seen. He has been consistently above and beyond and has been exceptional in every regard, stable and has been improving everyday. He is ready to take the reigns of his squad and monitors his squads guesting and overall ability. He takes care of his squad down to the molecule. He took leadership of prepping and moderating and building Dog company battle of the squads up from nothing without being asked. He loves the unit and his squad. He is smart, dedicated and although he will have to work into how he will now build himself as a Sergeant. Deb has proven time and time again to show the standards of a Sgt. He is always seen in drills and in teamspeak being active. I rely heavily on him to do things without being asked and to lead without supervision. His actions and dedication and knowledge of the 29th and leadership should be awarded and his rank should be more fitting of his character. That is why I am happy to see him get Sgt. You have come a long way my friend and you have a wide future open to you.

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Aristidi L. Deb       113468681       DP3S2

I do promote him to Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 19th day of April 2020 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
