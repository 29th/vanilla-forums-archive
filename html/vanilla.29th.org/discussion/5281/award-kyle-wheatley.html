Award (Kyle Wheatley)

Maj. Cooke [Ret.]
July 2015 in Promotions and Awards

Major Wheatley has been in the unit for over 8 years and the Battalion Executive Officer for over 2 years. During his time in the 29th, Maj. Wheatley has contributed immensely to the 29th in every facet possible, from clerking to office work to leading the unit through many changes.

Maj. Wheatley joined the unit back in 2006 with the 153rd Training Platoon (which brought us another absolutely amazing member of the 29th...whom I sure you all know and love...) and was assigned to CP2S1. In July 2007, CP4 was created and Maj. Wheatley took on the position as Platoon Clerk for the newly created platoon. After proving to be one of the best clerks in the 29th ID, then Technician, Fourth Grade Wheatley proved that the sword may be stronger than the pen when he took on the role of Platoon Sergeant for CP4. On 22 January 2008, Maj. Wheatley was commissioned as a Second Lieutenant and assigned as Platoon Leader of CP4.

While in command of CP4, Maj. Wheatley commanded his platoon in an official scrimmage before lending a significant hand in transitioning the 29th out of the dying Day of Defeat into Darkest Hour shortly after taking command of the newly created Baker Company. This was a difficult time for the whole 29th leadership team, but Maj. Wheatley led the way with a positive attitude and propelled Baker Company to the forefront of 29th combat abilities.

After becoming Battalion Executive Officer in January 2013, Maj. Wheatley has led the 29th through tough decisions and to greater heights. The toughest of which was expanded the 29th to three games. This was a goal that had been discussed extensively since 2008, but we had never been able to find the support and leadership; that is until Maj. Wheatley's influence.

Major Wheatley has proven to be an incredible resource to the 29th whose influence cannot be argued. His fingerprint can be seen across the 29th and I can say that, without a doubt, we would all be in a worse off place without Maj. Wheatley's leadership. Leading a group over many years through many changes is no easy task. There is no one in this unit more deserving of the Distinguished Service Cross!
