Promotion and Badges (Justin R. Santora)

T3 Grimes [Ret.]
July 2019 in Promotions and Awards

Cpl. Gibson said:

So I'm not going to lie, Pvt. Santora is a weird guy. Like seriously a weird dude. But thats okay, because I will always take someone who is weird, as long as they show up and try. And I can say that Santora does this and more. He has done an excellent job during his time in my squad at both killing and communicating. Early on in his time in the squad I made the comment after a platoon drill that Pvt. Santora made a huge impact, and while he didn't rack up a ton of kills, he communicated extremely well and was an instrumental part of our successful attack. Further into his time with us his kills did in fact increase and he truly has become a valuable member of the squad and I am very happy to have him. With that being said, I think a some stuff is necessary to represent these words on your jacket. So Pvt. Santora congratulations on receiving your Expert Infantry Badge and Rifle Marksman Badge. Oh and you are PFC Santora now. Congrats!

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Justin R. Santora       216594325      EP3S4

I do promote him to Private, First Class in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 30th day of July 2019 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
