Promotion (Alex W. Crane) 

T3 Grimes [Ret.]
October 2018 edited October 2018 in Promotions and Awards

SSgt. Robles said:

It's odd. I never imagined I would be writing a quote like this; more on this later. PFC Crane is an individual that most people in Easy Company know. He is one of the ASLs in EP3 and a well-known AR from his EP2 days. While most people know this, they may be surprised to know that he has had a long history in the 29th. Not only has he been in leadership in the past, he was even my SL at one point. So, like I said earlier, writing this quote is a bit odd. PFC Crane has always been a very charismatic individual; he hasn't changed at all. Due to this, when he was given an ASL spot again, he really flourished. He made it his mission to not only better his squad, but the platoon and even the company as well. He is the reason we have a pub night and are getting many more recruits. I'm glad to know that S1, under Sgt. Kelso and him, has a bright future to look forward to. Now, here I am, doing something I never thought that I would be doing: promoting him. PFC Crane will be promoted to the rank of Cpl. to better reflect his position within the unit. Congratulations, Cpl. Crane.

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Alex W. Crane       004825138      EP3S1

I do promote him to Corporal in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 23rd day of October 2018 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
