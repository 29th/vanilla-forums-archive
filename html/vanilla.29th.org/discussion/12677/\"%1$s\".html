Promotion (Alexander Novak)

Cpl. Saerens [Ret.]
July 2016 edited July 2016 in Promotions and Awards

SSgt. Part said:

Cpl. Novak joined the 29th in (2013-11-21) and has been in AP1 his whole career, he knows it inside and outside. He has seen the ups and downs of AP1. He took over Squad 1 as it's SL little over one year ago, he completed his SLT seven months ago and got promoted to the rank of Corporal at the same time, completing SLT came natural to him since he already had some experience in leadership. Now Squad 1 got weakened by people leaving and transfers for a short while, but I can proudly say that it's no more the case, through Novaks hard work and a new transfer into Squad 1 for the ASL position, the squad is stronger than ever.

He is one of my most experienced SLs and I can really count on him to get the job done. With his squad, he is with no doubt a force to be reckoned with. His success is a result of constant progress and his drive to become ever better. Considering all this and his impeccable reputation, Co. HQ and myself find it fit that the rank of Cpl no longer suits his stature. I'm honored to hereby present to you all: Sgt. Novak, congrats Sarge!

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Alexander Novak     077371294     AP1S1

I do promote him to Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 29th day of June 2016.

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
