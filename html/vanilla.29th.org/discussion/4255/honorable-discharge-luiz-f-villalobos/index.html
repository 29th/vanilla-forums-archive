Honorable Discharge (Luiz F. Villalobos)

Cpt. Hill [Ret.]
June 2015 edited June 2015 in Discharges

HONORABLE DISCHARGE

FROM THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

This is to certify that


Luiz F Villalobos    Sgt.   CP2S1

was Honorably Discharged from the

UNITED STATES ARMY

on the 8th day of June 2015. This certificate is awarded

as a testimonial of Honest and Faithful Service





ARPC-OPM-O

DATE: June 8 2015

MEMORANDUM FOR Commander,
U.S. Army 29th Infantry Division, ATTN: AHRC-OP-S, 1 Reserve Way
Fort Belvoir, VA 22060


SUBJECT: Unqualified Resignation


1.I hereby tender my unqualified resignation as an Enlisted Soldier in the
United States Army under the provisions of Chapter 6, Section III,
AR 135-175.

2.I understand that if my resignation is accepted I am entitled to an
honorable separation and will be furnished an Honorable Discharge
Certificate. I understand that I cannot resign if I am a statutory obligor.


Luiz F. Villalobos
Signature
Luiz F. Villalobos
Name (Type or Print)
Sgt. Asst. Squad Leader CP2S1
Rank Position Platoon/Squad
I’ve been in the 29th for 4 years, 8 months and 27 days, as of today. In that seemingly endless, yet finite time, I’ve heard all kinds of different analogies for what this unit represents from all kinds of different people from all of the little corners of earth. I’ll be the first to admit that I had no idea what to expect when I enlisted, my reasoning was: “I enjoy Darkest Hour, and this seems like a fun community, so I’ll join so I don’t lose interest in the game.” However, never I thought that little decision would bring me the joy it did and holded me here for over a quarter of my life. Which brings me to my analogy, a small realization I had during that period, the 29th is like a little and accelerated life. We all enter it with no knowledge of what to expect, and with no saying in what our new family will be. We’re instantly the little brother who has no idea what he is doing and likes to charge MGs (okey, I’ll admit the analogy doesn’t hold well here). But with time, we grow, we fight, we suffer and we gain experience until we’re ready to start and create our own little family, and pass our knowledge further. Though, like life, it must come to an end, and this is where I stand. For reasons that I cannot control, it will be impossible for me to continue to fulfill my duties, so it's time for me to part ways. It would be unfeasible for me to acknowledge and thank the countless people that were part of my road in this unit. However, I feel it is important for me to say that from this little life of mine, I’ll carry many friends and found memories to the rest of my of my days.
