Promotion & Award (Thomas Zanovich)

T4 Orhan
September 2016 edited November 2016 in Promotions and Awards

Sgt. Ternby said:

Pvt. Zanovich has now been in the 29th for a total of 11 months and since day one, he's impressed the entire Squad with his really accurate shots with the rifle. He's recently picked up the heavy and is performing on the level of the top guys in the squad, which is really incredible and great to see since we've got such a veteran squad! Not only is he a great shot, he's also come a long way with communication and cooperation with the squad and works really well together with everyone in the squad and has even tried some leading, with good results. He's grown to become a mature member of the unit and I can only see a bright future for a soldier that already is as skilled as he is and that also works as hard as he does to keep on improving. Simply put, he's without doubt one of the most impressive Pvt's I've seen in my 29th career and I don't see any reason for why he isn't ready to be promoted to PFC! Congratulations PFC Zanovich! Keep up the excellent work you're doing!.


2Lt. Dashwood said:

Recruitment is probably the most important thing to keep 29th alive. Pvt. Zanovich took the initiative and decided to step up for this important job. With his efforts, he managed to recruit two new recruits into 29th. It's my great pleasure to award him his First Combat Action Badge! Congratulations PFC!

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Thomas Zanovich      042759386     BP1S1

I do promote him to Private, First Class in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 15th day of September 2016.

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
