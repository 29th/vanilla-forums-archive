Promotion and Five Years and Six Months of Service (Adam G. Jacobsson)

CW4 Slomczynski
June 2017 in Promotions and Awards

Lt. Col. Wheatley said:

1Lt. Jacobsson has been with the 29th much longer than his tour of duty hints at. When I met him in late 2009, I knew he was going places and I have enjoyed watching him take his journey through the 29th. Whenever something is needed from 1Lt. Jacobsoon, he was and is always there to step up and get it done. In addition to always stepping up for the 29th, he works a full-time job, balances a fiance (planning a wedding), and even has time to create music with some sweet albums out already! It's like he's learned how to warp time and add hours into a day. I'm glad the offices have a stable foundation in 1Lt. Jacobsson and have been discussing the great work that he and the offices have been performing. As such, we decided that the rank of First Lieutenant just didn't match the level and quality of work that Jacobsson represented. That being said, it is my extreme pleasure to award the man who has followed in my footsteps in so many ways with my old rank while he serves in my old position, congratulations, CAPTAIN JACOBSSON! For his dedication to the unit, we are also awarding Cpt. Jacobsson with his Eleventh Army of Occupation Medal for Five Years and Six Months of Service. Here's to eleven more, years!

*Salute*

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Adam G. Jacobsson       008800802       S-1/S-3

I do promote him to CAPTAIN in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 18th day of June 2017 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
