  • PFC Burkardt [Ret.]
    October 2016

    For some first hand experience, I participated in the 3/4 day open beta and was pleasantly surprised by the refreshing atmosphere of the weaponry. The rifles and machine guns are "realistically" (i put quotes for relativity's sake) inaccurate and the general flow of the game feels like they're attempting to get back to the roots of Bad company. More Bang. More Grit. Less tediousness.

    That being said. Do not in any way expect this to be a true WW1 experience. Buy Verdun if you want that. This is flashy. Fast paced. Action oriented shooter. It does that well in my opinion. The saturation and gun play is extremely satisfying along with the melee system (clubs and bayonet stabs).

    Overall I believe battlefield 1 is a step in the right direction for Dice, I would recommend picking it up eventually to anyone who enjoys the Battlfield series.

    To wrap up, this game in essence is not intended to replicate World War 1's realism in anything except showy flashy maps, guns, and whatever else they have. That being said, battlefield 1 as the next BATTLEFIELD game I would say deserves praise for stepping in the right direction for the series. A bold move, with plenty of new content and settings to keep it going. Better than what CoD is doing in my humble opinion.
