Honorable Discharge (David H. Clyde )

FSgt. Balzer [Ret.]
September 2015 edited September 2015 in Discharges


HONORABLE DISCHARGE

FROM THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

This is to certify that

David H. Clyde     Sergeant    CP2S1

was Honorably Discharged from the

UNITED STATES ARMY

on the 29th day of SEPTEMBER 2015. This certificate is awarded

as a testimonial of Honest and Faithful Service





ARPC-OPM-O

DATE: 29 SEPT 2015

MEMORANDUM FOR Commander,

U.S. Army 29th Infantry Division, ATTN: AHRC-OP-S, 1 Reserve Way

Fort Belvoir, VA 22060


SUBJECT: Unqualified Resignation


1.I hereby tender my unqualified resignation as an Enlisted Soldier in the

United States Army under the provisions of Chapter 6, Section III,

AR 135-175.

2.I understand that if my resignation is accepted I am entitled to an

honorable separation and will be furnished an Honorable Discharge

Certificate. I understand that I cannot resign if I am a statutory obligor.


David H. Clyde
Signature
David H. Clyde
Name (Type or Print)
Sergeant Squad Leader Charlie Company, Second Platoon, First Squad
Rank Position Platoon/Squad

It comes time for me to have to hang my jacket up. I have been in the 29th since 2010. Over the years I have met many people and made many of friends. I have learned more than I even thought I would in the 29th. I strengthened my leadership and people skills in this unit. One key thing I learned is you can disagree with a point of view in many ways, one is with a smile which creates better results. This family helped me prepare for my next family in the military. I have always loved being in the 29th but as the years have gone by my availability has deteriorated. I now am in a relationship with the woman I will most inevitably marry. I have been made a Fire Team Leader in the US Army, which I now keep track of 11 other soldiers besides my self. I've been promoted at my job in the civilian world and I travel frequently and unexpectedly to represent my job across the nation. All of this couldn't have been possible with out my determination but also with out the guidance from those in the 29th that explained to me patience is a must and knowing when is the right time and place to stand out. It has been an honor serving with you all, from Privates to our Lieutenant Colonel. Maybe one day down the road, when I settle down I will return, I know for a fact the 29th ID will still be here when that day comes. Salute Ever Foward! Sgt. Clyde
