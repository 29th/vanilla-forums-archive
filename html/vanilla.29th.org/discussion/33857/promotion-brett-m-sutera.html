Promotion (Brett M. Sutera)

T5 Perez Pantoja
September 2019 edited September 2019 in Promotions and Awards

TSgt. Dethfield said:

"It's times like these that make me wonder where the time actually went. In January 2018, I was assigned to lead the newly formed EP2S4 mere months after the unit had just expanded into Squad. Times were turbulent but exciting and I was eager to get back into the role. Among the first batch of new privates I received was Pvt. Sutera, a mild-mannered fellow with a good attitude but a little lacking in confidence. This would change quickly and even though he may have actually soiled himself when I first thrust him into leadership during a squad drill, I could tell he had great potential if just... grew a pair. Well, today I can see he did, indeed, grow a pair. We are now a little over a year since my time as the SL of EP2S4 and we have here Cpl. Sutera, squad leader of the second iteration of EP2S4. He took the opportunity to lead a squad on his own and overnight had his squad become one of our strongest. Maybe it was the fact he even kept the original name, maybe it was my constant badgering and pressuring him. But in all honesty, it was just Cpl. Sutera being the driven NCO we all hoped he would be. EP4 is currently looking a little too Corporal-heavy for my taste, which makes Sutera's promotion to Sergeant surprisingly convenient. Excellent work, Sgt. Sutera."

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Brett M. Sutera       131103788       EP4S1

I do promote him to Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 1st day of September 2019 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
