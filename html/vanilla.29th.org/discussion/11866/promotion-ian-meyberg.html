Promotion (Ian Meyberg)

T4 Burg
May 2016 in Promotions and Awards

SSgt. Vandewalle said:

Corporal Meyberg is now with us for his forth tour within the unit. His original enlistment dates back from 2010. Since then he has accumulated more than 4 years and a half in active duty.

During these periods of time, Corporal Meyberg always stood up and helped out where needed. Wherever he is placed, he shows great effort and professionalism.
And so I have noticed myself. I've been putting Corporal Meyberg to the test in many different circumstances. He has been excelling in each of those situations. Whether it was a disciplinary action, attacking a fortified position or just getting the job done, Corporal Meyberg is always there. He's a solid squad leader which aims for perfection on every aspect of his position. He is also a reliable fall back for platoon leadership. Always there to help out where needed.
Due to Corporal Meyberg's skill and effort as a squad leader, I am more than proud to promote him to the rank of Sergeant.

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Ian Meyberg      000556481     CP2S2

I do promote him to Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 22th day of May 2016.

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
