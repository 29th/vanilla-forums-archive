Promotion (Sean P. Mortem) 

T3 Grimes [Ret.]
August 2020 in Promotions and Awards

SSgt. Crane said:

There's something special about Corporal Mortem. From the very first time I met the man, pubbing on Sumari, I had a sense that he'd do great in the 29th. As he stepped into SLT, he proved himself ferociously determined. When he ascended to take the place of ASL, he demonstrated unrelenting dedication to the role. The moment the position of Squad Leader was thrust upon him, Corporal Mortem thrived. Every single milestone Mortem met, Mortem exceeded. As his previous SL, his recruiter, and his friend I cannot possibly overstate his successes. Unsurprisingly, then, we find ourselves at one more milestone. After nearly half a year of service in the position of squad leader, Corporal Mortem has at last proven without a shadow of a doubt that he is deserving of more. With that in mind, I hereby have the honor of promoting him to the rank of Sergeant in the 29th Infantry Division. Congratulations, Sergeant, I can't begin to imagine what your next milestone will be.

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Sean P. Mortem       253915669      EP3S1

I do promote him to Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 2nd day of August 2020 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
