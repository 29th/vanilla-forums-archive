Promotion and Award (Sasha Greeves) 

PFC Noonan
January 2020 in Promotions and Awards

WO1 Bruneau said:

PFC Greeves here has been an outstanding and remarkable member since the day he joined AP3S4 in ye olden days. I have had the pleasure of knowing this spry and plucky young guy ever sense his first day in the unit. Unfortunately for us, he had to take a bit of a sabbatical as real life gets hectic. This did not stop me from insistently bugging him about his return to the 29th. Lucky for me my wish came true, and he wandered back into my grasp, this time under new pretenses. I was his superior. It was my job to make him grow, and grow did this boy ever. Greeves pushed himself to be more assertive and more demanding. He knew what he had to offer, and he wanted to see what others had in them. It is this "Ever Forward" mentality that he brings that fills me with pride and joy. His indomitable spirit and conviction is an example for any member of leadership. As he has completed all of his SLT tasks and lessons and has proven himself to be a dedicated and driven leader, it is no longer fair to call him PFC. Congratulations, Corporal Grevees. Stay the path you are on. The bar has risen, and I know you can meet the challenge.

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Sasha Greeves       053222703      DP3S1

I do promote him to Corporal in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 14th day of January 2020 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
