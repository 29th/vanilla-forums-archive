  • 2Lt. Bergstrom
    June 2015

    Okay I just talked about this with my roommate so I'm all worked up.

    Here's my problems with Battlefront as I've seen it so far:
    DICE can't seem to think outside the movies - to the point of forgetting what happened in the movies. What I mean by this is they can't get away from the movies on seemingly any level. Battle of Hoth? Obviously you need a counter for Vader on Hoth, why not a post-Dagobah strength Luke (not only is he able to shove groups of men when the last time we saw him on Hoth he LITERALLY HAD TO FOCUS TO MOVE A TINY LIGHTSABER but he's wearing the same outfit HE WORE IN ROTJ)? Also - you're on Hoth and during the "Walker Assault" gameplay.. your AT-AT can call in an "orbital bombardment". WHAT? You mean the thing that the PLANETARY DEFENSE SHIELD is supposed to protect the planet from?! THE SHIELD POWERED BY THE GENERATOR YOU YOURSELF ARE SUPPOSED TO DESTROY. I mean that's just.. wow. This is the level of thought being put into this game?


    "The field is strong enough to deflect any bombardment."

    Now okay, sure that's just the trailer.. maybe I'm overreacting. (Hey, it's the internet) Maybe it's just Hoth that is nonsensical and terrible (on second thought let's not go to Camelot for it is a silly place). What other 12 maps can we expect? As of release we know 2 of the 3 are from the movies (Endor and the aforementioned/raged Hoth) with the last being the as of yet unseen on the big screen Sullust. But I swear to Obi-wan .. if I see that the 12 maps are mostly movies related I'm gonna choke a Hutt.

    There is absolutely no reason for us to be playing on Tatooine. None. It's a backwater that had nothing going on - hence Luke being bored and wanting to leave. The fact that Lucas, his brain long since withered and blown away decided to stage Episode 1 on Tatooine, should not be considered at this stage. Tatooine at the time of this game, should be the galactic equivalent of Napanee, Ontario. I mean that's just basic storyline. When the biggest excitement is going in to Toshi Station to pick up some power converters, you don't have a Team Death Match of Rebels and Imperials. Using maps from the movies is just being lazy and in my opinion doing a total disservice to the Star Wars Universe as a whole. Also if I see Naboo...


    The booming metropolis of Mos Eisley

    While on the subject of the ridiculous, the level of "ease of accessibility" is highly disconcerting from a replay value and skills progression perspective. Chances are if you're playing Star Wars Batllefront, or just like the Star Wars Universe, you've played some other Star Wars game. (my Star Wars gaming CV is long and distinguished). Yes I'm an ARMA guy, so having 18 different buttons and combos isn't something that shatters my brain. That being said.. if you're going to have X-Wings and TIE fighters you need to play X-Wing Alliance at the very least so you know what it's like in an Incom T-65b (XWing). Of course if you're going to have X-wings and TIEs it only makes sense to have space battles. Sure it's not promised in the release (or I believe any further in the mentioning of it) but it's Star Wars. I don't want those stars to merely be a backdrop to my terrestrial bound combat. I mean to use the Holy Trilogy, the opening of A New Hope took place in a Blockade Running boarding action (I'm not saying - hey let's play as Yoda defending Princess Leia's Corellian Corvette - sweet Frank Oz no) and it was pretty damn awesome. Plus shipboard combat doesn't need half the environmental effects you get on a planet map so from a mapmaking perspective it seems like that'd be easier as well..


    Your ship here

    Maps again. So the way EA has done their Battlefield DLC maps is bass ackward. It divides the community by making you pay for maps to play. This means your player base is split and servers running complete maplists are going to be kicking players who don't own the DLC. You are going to actively be booting your player base and thus preventing them from playing. Will this be rectified for Battlefront? Maps need to be for everyone and if you want DLC you make it customization character junk. Have a Bothan head for your character. Maybe a purely cosmetic bandoleer or gunslinger holster for your blaster. Will EA go this route for making more money from this title? Or will they instead hamstring the servers/player base? Alas I feel that is a pessimistically rhetorical question.


    C-3PO: No, I don't think he likes you at all.
    [R2 beeps]
    C-3PO: No, I don't like you either

    So that's my rant exhausted. I'm sure there will be more facts and developments as time passes and maybe (hopefully) some of my fears and concerns will be allayed. Likely I'll just pass on this one and wait for the next. One thing that is satisfying is the knowledge that the Star Wars IP is out of Lucas' hands and now we can see more variety and creative projects come to fruition (provided Disney isn't as tightly hamfisted). So though this likely won't be the Star Wars I want, it'll be a stepping stone to the one I do (a new X-Wing/Dark Forces esque shooter would be lovely thanks).
