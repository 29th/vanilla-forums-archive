Promotion and Award (Anthony R. Hardy)

T5 Brown [Ret.]
May 2019 edited May 2019 in Promotions and Awards

SSgt. Butler said:

For the past few months, PFC Hardy has been put to the test time and time again. He's been thrown more curve-balls in that time than most squad leaders ever see. He's handled them with grace and maturity and always with the intention of finding the best outcome. While his total time spent in SLT was not necessarily smooth, he stands before you now as proof that persistence, hard work and self-awareness will help you go the distance. Tonight, he was put to the final test of his SLT programme and after much deliberation, we've come to a decision. Having displayed the skill, composure and leadership example we expect of all our NCO's, PFC Hardy has proved his mettle. Now, his leadership journey truly begins. With all this said, it is our utmost pleasure to announce that, henceforth, he shall be known as PFC Hardy no more.

Congratulations, Cpl. Hardy. You've worked hard for this and have proved your worth for all present to see.

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Anthony R. Hardy       005394510      CP2S1

I do promote him to Corporal in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 5th day of May 2019 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
