T4 Burg
December 2020 edited December 2020 in Promotions and Awards



29TH INFANTRY DIVISION
116TH REGIMENT, 1ST BN
CHARLIE COMPANY

Official Awards for Meritorious Service Before and During CP1's Deployment

15th NOVEMBER, 2020


First Lieutenant Patrick D. Nelson

1st Lt. Nelson has participated in his second official scrimmage. For winning the attack round during an official scrimmage he will receive the European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal. In addition, for leading the Charlie Platoon One section to victory in an official scrimmage 2Lt. Nelson will receive the Legion of Merit.


Second Lieutenant David Drake

2nd Lt. Drake has participated in his first official scrimmage and will receive the Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. For winning the attack round during an official scrimmage he will receive the European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal. In addition, for leading the combined FNF coalition to victory in an official scrimmage Sgt. Drake will receive the Legion of Merit.


Staff Sergeant Jim Halls

SSgt. Halls has participated in his first official scrimmage and will receive the Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. For winning the attack round during an official scrimmage he will receive the European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal.

SSgt. Halls distinguished himself in combat by ably assisting Sgt. Drake in leading the company to victory, often taking personal command of important flanks while the company pressed its main assaults. SSgt. Halls was also involved in all aspects of company-level planning for this operation. As acting XO, he developed OPFOR plans for both rounds that helped Sgt. Drake and the platoon leaders select the appropriate strategy. He also made himself available for multiple planning meetings and briefings leading up to the event. SSgt. Halls also assisted Sgt. Drake in creating drill plans that prepared the men for deployment. Although SSgt. Halls lost his life personally commanding an infantry charge on the first objective, his capable planning and exemplary performance in Company HQ during the fight secured victory.

Technician, 5th Grade Chan Ho Bae

T/5 Bae has participated in his first official scrimmage and will receive the Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. For winning the attack round during an official scrimmage he will receive the European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal.

T/5 Bae distinguished himself in combat action by providing critical supporting action to 1st Platoon and Bravo. During the attack on the second objective, company command came under heavy marksman fire from the rear. T/5 Bae maneuvered on and eliminated the enemy marksman under the cover of withering suppression fire from 1st Platoon. Sustaining heavy injuries, T/5 Bae’s actions undoubtedly secured the company’s rear and saved the company commander’s life. He also clutched the round win by expertly driving 1st Platoon (in a captured enemy vehicle) into the final objective with less than a minute and a half on the clock. Surviving the battle, his actions shored up the attack at every step of the way, significantly contributing to the victory.



Private, First Class Eric C. Nielsen

PFC Nielsen has participated in his first official scrimmage and will receive the Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. For winning the attack round during an official scrimmage he will receive the European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal.

PFC Nielsen distinguished himself in combat action both as a soldier and a leader. First, PFC Nielsen saved the life of a heavily injured 2Lt. Nelson, who lived to assume command of 1st Platoon and maintain the attack. PFC Nielsen also pulled an injured Sgt. Dott out of the line of fire in the initial ambush. Second, PFC Nielsen, who was already on squad net as squad medic, exemplified the highest values of the 29th ID when he took competent field command of the Bravo element for the rest of the round and, though injured, survived until the round end. His leadership enabled Bravo to anchor 1st Platoon actions on the first and second objectives despite sustaining heavy casualties. Surviving until the end of the fight, PFC Nielsen’s leadership set up the remnants of 1st Platoon and Bravo to secure the final objective with minutes to spare.


Private, First Class Nathan W. Wells

PFC Wells has participated in his first official scrimmage and will receive the Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. For winning the attack round during an offical scrimmage he will receive the European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal.

PFC Wells (Bravo 1 Medic) distinguished himself in combat as both a soldier and as a supporting element by performing critical actions that helped 1st Platoon win the initial ambush engagement and secure the first objective. In addition to killing one enemy attacker while getting injured in the initial ambush, PFC Wells flawlessly performed his duties as a combat medic, saving the lives of several soldiers, including squad leaders, around him. The list of lives PFC Wells saved includes Sgt. Robertson, Sgt. Dott, PFC B. Williams, and three members of other units in 1st Platoon. PFC Wells also killed another enemy during the attack on the first objective. While PFC Wells and many of the soldiers he saved did not ultimately survive the battle, his swift and skillful actions to save their lives kept 1st Platoon and Bravo at fighting strength to secure victory. For his action, he also receives the Purple Heart.

    FNF vs. Red Bear SCRIM AAR:

    Round 1 Attack (Ruha)

    On November 15, 2020, the 29th Infantry Division deployed to Ruha with FNF Forces attack three terminals defended by Red Bear Forces in sequential order. In the first two minutes of the operation, 29th forces came under a sustained enemy attack by pre-ranged explosive ordnance and high-caliber weapons fire. Caught unprepared, 29th forces and the rest of the company fought back to successfully overcome the enemy ambush. When the dust settled, the enemy had sustained heavier casualties than the 29th and had lost their BTR. Due to disorganization caused by the initial ambush, the company dismounted farther from the objective than desired, and the enemy, realizing their failed ambush, moved to harass and slow friendly forces through the dense vegetation. Despite slow movement and conditions favoring the enemy, the company responded to all contact with overwhelming fire, pressed the attack, and captured a heavily-defended Terminal 1. Moving south to attack Terminal 2, 29th forces and their platoon provided cover and base-of-fire for 2nd Platoon, which attacked the objective from the south. As the vicious CQC fight tipped in favor of FNF forces on Terminal 2, the remnants of 29th forces and their 1st Platoon comrades mounted a captured enemy truck and rushed to take what turned out to be an undefended Terminal 3 with less than a minute and a half left on the clock. Even though enemy tactics slowed the company’s advance and almost ran down the clock, a combination of cohesive movement, overwhelming firepower, and improvised mobility won the day with seconds to spare.

    Round 2 Defense (Takistan)

    On November 15, 2020, the 29th Infantry Division deployed to Takistan with FNF Forces to defend three terminals from attack by Red Bear Forces in sequential order. 29th forces were tasked with defending the northeastern flank of the ridgeline anchoring the Terminal 1 defense. Red Bear made a three-pronged attack. On the west, a motorized team deployed to Terminals 2 and 3. On the east, the enemy BTR and several motorized teams engaged in a running battle with FNF vics and 29th forces. And in the south, the enemy pushed an infantry team into Terminal 1. As soon as contacts were called on Terminal 1, 1st Platoon began to fall back to Terminal 2 and 3 as planned. At that exact moment, the server experienced a lag spike (yellow link). While FNF forces held in place to let the server catch up, Red Bear eliminated most 29th forces (undetected for several minutes due to lag). When the server caught back up, those who had been killed dropped dead and the terminals appeared to have been activated simultaneously. As all remaining forces fell back, SSgt. Halls and PFC Montogmery were part of an ad hoc team that stormed Terminal 3 and killed the lone enemy defender just as the terminal clock ran out. They were about five seconds away from pausing the terminal and winning the round. At desync, FNF forces had eliminated both enemy BTRs while preserving their own M1117. 1st Platoon had already begun to fallback to Terminal 2 and 3, pausing only due to desync. While the result stands at technical loss, it is very possible that were it not for desync, the round might have resulted in a win.
