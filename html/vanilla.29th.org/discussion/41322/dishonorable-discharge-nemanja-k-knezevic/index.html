Dishonorable Discharge (Nemanja K. Knezevic)

Technician, 4th Grade Adam K. Stachurski
October 2020 edited August 2022 in Discharges

ARPC-OPM-O

DATE: 21ST October 2020

MEMORANDUM FOR Commander,

U.S. Army 29th Infantry Division, ATTN: AHRC-OP-S, 1 Reserve Way

Fort Belvoir, VA 22060


SUBJECT: Notice of Military Discharge


DISHONORABLE
Category of discharge


The following soldier of the US Army in the 29th Infantry Division has received a military discharge and is relieved of duty.

  1. The dischargee is not welcome in our server or forums.
  2. The dischargee may no longer wear the 29th Infantry Division Tags.
  3. The dischargee may not reenlist.


Signature
Nemanja K. Knezevic
Name (Type or Print)
Sergeant Squad Leader Dog Company, First Platoon, First Squad
Rank Position Platoon/Squad

All in all, it has been a pleasure. I was hoping that I would be able to at least stay a bit longer, for nothing at least to say goodbye to my squad mates in person, and the other people who I shared thick and thin, after the squad or platoon drill, but it is how it is, I just can't attend those days sadly. Again, I am not saying that I am done with 29th for good, as one can not know what future brings, but, after some time, if everything goes well, I will see to re-enlist. Sadly, that won't be any time soon, I am afraid, not in this year, nor the next. With working in the morning, then traveling quite a large distance for university to listen to lectures, and getting back home late at night, one is either totally spent, or, late to the drills. And those who know me know that I am a man of my word, and I gave myself the word that I will get masters degree as quickly as possible. When, if, lockdown happens again, or if I do not go at university one way or the other, I am extremely busy at home workshop, working up until late at night every working day. It is hard to believe that there are people with their entire 29th careers who only served under me, well, under me from now, and to be fair I would hardly believe that I would end up being what I am back in 2016 when I enlisted. At least, I am glad that I leave behind me one stable platoon, and very reliable squad, with really good members, from our first, to our last. And I am glad I kept my word to the end. You all are exceptionally good individuals, and do not let anyone tell you otherwise. Keep working on yourself, both personal, real life wise, and 29th wise, make yourself capable and reliable persons, and rest will follow. Farewell, gentlemen. Ever forward!

Reason for leaving
