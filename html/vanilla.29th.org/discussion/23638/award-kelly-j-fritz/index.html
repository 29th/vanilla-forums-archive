Award (Kelly J. Fritz)

Col. Wheatley
January 2018 in Promotions and Awards

Reworking a long established procedure in the 29th is no easy task. When it came to overhauling the entire Officer Candidate School, it was known that Battalion Headquarters was asking for a lot of work, determination, and patience. Following a review of the program with 2Lt. Bergstrom, Cpt. Fritz was asked to implement the new procedures during 2017 with a new cadre of OCS Candidates. This implementation led to the highest number of OCS graduates in one year ever, leading to five new Platoon Leaders with shiny bars of butter on their shoulders. Not only did we increase our graduation efficiency, but thanks to the work and efforts of Cpt. Fritz, we were able to retain our high standards of quality leadership.

It is stated that one is not truly proficient in a task until they are able to teach someone else said task. Not only was Cpt. Fritz able to rework a few documents in theory, he was able to teach and promote leadership across the entire Officer Corps. There was only one commendation that Battalion Headquarters felt was appropriate for the level of success demonstrated by Cpt. Fritz and that was the second highest medal that can be earned by a 29th member, the French Croix De Guerre! This is only the third time this medal has been awarded and is the first in over ten years.

I want to personally thank Cpt. Fritz for all of the hard work he has put into Officer Candidate School over the last year or two, it truly has made great benefits to the whole of the Battalion. 29th, Ever Forward, Captain! I can't wait to see where your next project takes you.
