Technician, 5th Grade Joshua S. Evarts

T/5 Evarts has participated in his second official scrimmage. For winning the defence round during an offical scrimmage he will the Defense Meritorious Service Medal.

T/5 Evarts began the round on the far south in a scouting position in case the enemy attempted to flank around us from that direction. He did not have to wait long however as it turns out the enemy sent 2 of their squads to attack the objective area from the direct south, which put T/5 Evarts slightly behind them as they advanced between T/5 Evarts and T/5 Perez Pantoja who was nearby. Evarts got a kill early on into the round, but had to immediately fall back to avoid retaliation from the enemy. After a couple of kills from T/5 Perez the enemy finally decided that the south wasn't worth it anymore and decided to break off to the east and try again from there with support of the Bradley. T/5 Evarts fed this information to the rest of the team through command chat which allowed 2Lt. Dahl to command EP4 to respond appropriately. As the enemy began their new attack, EP4S1 came under pressure in their village SE of the objective. T/5 Evarts moved closer to help EP4S1 and quickly dispatched two enemies on the south side of the village. This combined with efforts from T/5 Perez and the BMP-2 caused the eastern attack to falter and fall apart as it pushed into the rest of EP4. Seeing that his job there was done, T/5 Evarts then relocated to the other side of the map where contacts remained in the NW in order to aid EP4S2. He found a position on a cliff overwatching some of the hills in that area and was able to kill one enemy at a distance, then immediately defend himself from another that happened to be nearby. Despite getting wounded, he managed to kill the second enemy and push on further to the west. From here he shadowed the enemy on the west side as they pushed in, relaying information and taking a few shots at the remaining enemies until the end of the round.


Technician, 5th Grade Drake H. Perez Pantoja

T/5 Perez Pantoja has participated in his first official scrimmage and will receive the Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. For winning the defence round during an offical scrimmage he will the Defense Meritorious Service Medal.

T/5 Perez Pantoja put in an extreme amount of effort during EP4's scrim preparation, so much so that he stood head and shoulders above his peers in that regard. He spent many nights after hours scouting the map and approaches for lanes to avoid and spots to use. He discovered a position for the Bradley in the NE, which allowed the IFV to safely destroy 2 of the 4 enemy objectives from afar. This became a central piece of EP4’s attack and was carried out successfully during the scrim, despite the rest of the team not being able to secure the other 2 objectives, his spot worked like a charm when it was needed. T/5 Perez was also a heavy presence on the theory crafting side of the prep as we formulated our plan. Without a doubt he was the MVP of EP4's preparation for the scrim and as such it is fitting that he be awarded a Soldier's Medal for his efforts.

Private, First Class George D. Logan

PFC Logan has participated in his third official scrimmage and will receive the Combat Infantry Badge, 2nd Award. For winning the defence round during an offical scrimmage he will the Defense Meritorious Service Medal.

PFC Logan was deployed on the far northern side of the defense with EP4S3 as one of their CE's. He was in a forward position in the north to act as an early warning system for the north side and potentially do some damage. PFC Logan fulfilled both of those missions wonderfully. Early on in the round he secured a double kill with a frag rocket which happened to kill the enemy team leader. This was one of the first casualties inflicted onto the attackers and no doubt caused damage to their entire team's effectiveness to perform. After the rest of the squad that was attacking the north side got into position and began firing at our team PFC Logan managed to get another kill and causing them to cut their losses at 3 and try to push further west, around Logan, and try to attack from that angle instead. However, once their position was reported several minutes after they broke contact Logan once again managed to land another frag rocket, this time from close to 400m out resulting in his 4th kill of the round. This was just short of half of the forces that attacked EP4 from the north side. Eventually though SquadOps was able to move up to his farmhouse through the forest and were able to overrun his position and kill him

  • T3 Grimes [Ret.]
    November 2020

    On Friday, October 16th, 2020, the members of Easy Company, Fourth Platoon, led by 2Lt. Dahl deployed against Squad Ops. The two forces engaged on the hillsides and riverside village of Gorodok, fighting to destroy two separate ammo boxes and Kornet emplacements (two located in both the North and South compounds).

    Attack Round

    Deploying within an industrial complex roughly 2km Southeast of the main objective area, EP4 mounted into their assigned vehicles and began driving North. After passing the radio tower that was located to the Eastern side across the river of the objective area, the team split up as their plan was threefold. First, the Bradley, crewed by FSgt. Dethfield, TSgt. Robles, and PFC Maestas, would be tasked with eliminating the Northern two objectives by shelling from a distance. Second, the squads would get into position to cross the river: S1 in the middle, S2 in the South, and S3 in the North. Third, the squads would make their way to the Southern compound and destroy the South objectives with the assumption that the North was already eliminated by the Bradley crew; if needed, they would sweep North. All vehicles got into position and the first shots were fired by the Bradley. While the Bradley was shelling the assigned objectives, the enemy BMP-2 was able to get an angle from the North area of the map, managing to kill Sgt. Newton almost immediately. Fortunately, the Bradley crew was able to knock out both Northern objectives in a little over a minute, while also eliminating the enemy TL that was stationed on the Northern Kornet. Unfortunately, however, the gunner, TSgt. Robles crashed and left the vehicle to be two-manned.

    EP4 began its assault across the river. S1 and S2 pushed closer together down the middle and South while S3 pushed North across the peninsula. At the time of this movement, the enemy BMP-2 had positioned itself in a spot where it could see down the river from the Northern cliffs. It was able to eliminate multiple people from S1; however, the Bradley was able to maneuver into a position to eliminate the BMP-2 almost immediately. As the BMP-2 was destroyed, S3 was able to push West into enemy territory. This, however, resulted in a terrible outcome for them as the majority of the squad was eliminated as they were too far North to receive accurate covering fire from the vehicle and other squads.

    Once across the river, the push came to a complete halt. For about four minutes, S1, S2, and the remains of S3 skirmished with the enemy to the West. During that time, EP4 was able to eliminate a good amount of the initial defenders; however, as more time passed by more of the Squad Ops defenders came to reinforce the front that had developed just West of the river. At this point, S3 was all but wiped out and S1 and S2 had sustained major casualties. The Bradley had moved in-between S1 and S2 at this point into what was known as the "Green Valley" but unfortunately, PFC Maestas crashed, leaving FSgt. alone in the vehicle. It wouldn't last much longer as FSgt. was killed trying to repair the tracked IFV. At this point, S1 and S2 ha pushed deeper West but were surrounded on all sides. One by one, the members of EP4 were picked off until only 2Lt. Dahl remained. Although he valiantly tried to push towards the objective, he was gunned down from multiple directions. Although a loss for EP4, they had managed to destroy 2/4 of the objectives; 14 Squad Ops defenders remained.

    Defense Round

    EP4 deployed itself to cover all of the areas around the two objective compounds. About five minutes after the round had gone live, the first rounds that were fired were from the enemy Bradley. It had positioned itself to the SE across the river in order to shoot at the Northern objectives like the former round. While similar to EP4's plan, it was positioned a lot more South and it was only able to destroy the Northern Kornet; the ammo box remained.

    A few more minutes passed and it was called out that the enemy was moving in from the SE across the river. S1 had been sent to the Southern village in order to protect against this push. T/5 Evarts was one of the first EP4 members to engage with the attacking force. Throughout this early engagement, T/5 Evarts was able to maneuver well and make many callouts while also eliminating multiple enemy targets. As the Southern front began to develop, Squad Ops began its attack from the Norther, both on the Western and Eastern flanks. It was about this time that PFC Logan, who was stationed there from the beginning to help reinforce S3, began to callout enemy targets. He soon used his launcher to dispatch enemies as the round went on. Fortunately, he was able to effectively kill the enemy TL with one of his frag rounds.

    As the round developed, the BMP, which was crewed by the same people as the attack round, was called to the South as the Bradley was seen crossing in that direction. Due to S1 holding their Southern village area well, Squad Ops decided to disengage from the SE and push North from the Eastern flank. Seeing this, 2Lt. Dahl called the majority of S1 back towards the objective in order to reinforce the Eastern Flank. The BMP positioned itself a little more North in order to also watch said flank. With a callout by T/5 Perez-Pantoja, the Bradley was able to be accurately tracked as it made its way to the North. Once on the direct East, the attackers began to slowly push West towards the objective area (similar to where EP4 attacked from the previous round). This, however, was countered quickly as the Bradley pushed forward without infantry support and was quickly destroyed by the BMP-2 in seconds. With the Bradley eliminated, the BMP-2 focused its attention on the Eastern flank and eliminated multiple enemy targets. The members of Squad Ops that weren't killed pushed NE to meet up with the few remaining attackers there. They were quickly eliminated, but not before landing a strike on the BMP-2 with one of their CEs. At this point, PFC Maestas crashed, leaving the vehicle to be two-manned.

    At this point, the action had settled down and the few remaining attackers were in the NW. They had to push through the woods located there, through S2, in order to reach the objective area. With S2 having sustained no casualties up to this point, the BMP-2 focusing its attention to that direction, and members of S1 looking that way from the South, the remaining Squad Ops attackers were relatively quickly eliminated. EP4 had won the defense by only losing one of the four objectives; 21 EP4 defenders remained.
