Ten Years of Service (Kyle J. Wheatley)

CSM Conrad
August 2017 in Promotions and Awards

I've let this posting go overdue for almost two months now. I've told myself its for various reasons. I certainly like to torture people who ask for me to do things by putting it off every time they ask, but I don't know that that truly applied here. But how does one go about writing an AOCC for the man who has dictated the path of the 29th for the last two years, and has been a vital member of the unit for a decade. It was easy to write my own, because God knows I can stroke my own ego. But Lieutenant Colonel is a different case.

I've known him the entire time he has been in the unit. We've always been close, always keeping tags on each other, and always pushing each other to be better. Rivals from the start, and friends to the end. I'm proud to say, unironically, that Lieutenant Colonel Wheatley is my brother and friend. The unit would not be the same without him, and we're all lucky to have him at the head of this fantastic community. I'm going to wrap this up before I start waxing philosophical or get sappy.

Lt. Col. Wheatley is receiving his 5th World War I Victory Medal and his 20th Army of Occupation Medal.
