Commission (Scott Dashwood)

CW4 Slomczynski
June 2016 edited June 2016 in Promotions and Awards

Cpt. Patty said:

Dashwood's second and final OCS Scrimmage was always going to be a challenging and nerve racking affair. However with excellent positioning and a detailed brief his forces set to work immediately in taking the German Village and winning Dashwood his commission.

Though taking early fire and losing several men during the crossing of the wooded area leading up to the village; the well planned approach routes and co-ordinated suppressive fire enabled Dashwood's team to quickly close in on the town and encircle the defenders.

Keeping a cool head Dashwood kept order and control, without him the assault would of surely gone to chaos in the dark and urban environment. With impressive skill the attackers closed in on all sides, quickly taking both objectives while inflicting heavy losses on the enemy with moderate losses themselves.

Once the objectives had been taken the orders to repulse the counter attack was given in good time. The enemy force was repelled seemingly with ease, though this was in no small part due to the supremely effective leadership. Once the attack had been repulsed Dashwood ordered an offensive; taking out the last two enemies in the Church, clearing the town, and completing his OCS scrim with all objectives achieved.

For completing OCS, performing excellently in his official scrimmage and impressing all of HQ and those who serve under him...

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of


SCOTT DASHWOOD      Corporal      BP2S1

I do promote him to Second Lieutenant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 18th day of June 2016.

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
