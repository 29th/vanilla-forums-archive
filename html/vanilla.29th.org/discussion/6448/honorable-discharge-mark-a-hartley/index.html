Honorable Discharge (Mark A. Hartley)

CW4 Slomczynski
September 2015 edited September 2015 in Discharges

Cpt. Patterson said:

SSgt Hartley is the most senior Platoon Sergeant in Baker Company, he is also among the best SNCOs in the 29th. Over his many years of service he has proven countless times his dedication to to the unit, and his service record is one that all NCOs in the 29th should aspire to.

Living in Australia means challenging drill times; in SSgt. Hartley's case Platoon drills fall at 4 AM. While making 4 AM Drills on weekdays is commendable in it's self SSgt. Hartley takes it to the next level, hosting weekly SLT seminars and platoon meetings before and after drills as-well as regularly attending BP1's squad drills too. It is this level of drive to better his Platoon and unit that defines SSgt. Hartley's time in the unit.

A classic NCO, Hartley says what he thinks and does not have a tendency of sugar coating things, however he always speaks the truth and his observance and sharp eye have both helped shape his platoon and indeed Baker Company in its entirety. While his high standards may seem tough to an outsider he is always willing to put in the time to help those with the motivation to better the unit and he has always been at the helm guiding his platoon to achieve his high standards.

Under his guidance he has raised an excellent Platoon Sergeant in SSgt. Kruitof who is now more than ready and prepared to take over the running of BP1 HQ in its entirety, and with SSgt. Hartley now returning to New Zealand he feels he is ready to take a break from the 29th, for a while at least. SSgt. Hartley will leave a strong, extremely positive legacy in Baker but I know due to his example and teachings BP1 is in safe hands.

Thanks for all your effort SSgt, and until your return we will have a spot in Baker waiting for you.

*Salute*

HONORABLE DISCHARGE

FROM THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

This is to certify that


MARK A. HARTLEY    Staff Sergeant    BP1 HQ

was Honorably Discharged from the

UNITED STATES ARMY

on the 8th day of September 2015. This certificate is awarded

as a testimonial of Honest and Faithful Service





ARPC-OPM-O

DATE: 8th Sep 2015

MEMORANDUM FOR Commander,

U.S. Army 29th Infantry Division, ATTN: AHRC-OP-S, 1 Reserve Way

Fort Belvoir, VA 22060


SUBJECT: Unqualified Resignation


1.I hereby tender my unqualified resignation as an Enlisted Soldier in the

United States Army under the provisions of Chapter 6, Section III,

AR 135-175.

2.I understand that if my resignation is accepted I am entitled to an

honorable separation and will be furnished an Honorable Discharge

Certificate. I understand that I cannot resign if I am a statutory obligor.


Signature
MARK A. HARTLEY

From SSgt. Hartley:

I will start off by saying the years I have spent in the 29th have been awesome enjoyed every minute of it..met some great guys from all over the world wouldn't have missed it for quids.
But the time has come that I must part ways with the unit, my family at this time need my support and it means heading home. As far as 29th duties are concerned the unit has advanced in caliber of the servers we provide but in doing so I have been left behind. The ping is too much for me to handle now and the enjoyment is curtailing because of it.
Of course I could continue in what I truly enjoy and that is looking after the guys under my command, but I am satisfied that my exec, SSgt. Kruithof, has it well in hand. He is a fine Platoon Sergeant and I have no doubt he will continue where I left off.
I would like to send my regards to 1Lt. Browning and Cpt. Patterson, two very fine officers. I know I have learnt something from both and I am sure they would say the same...
To 1st Platoon: we started off 60 guys in the change over and we whittled it down to 35 dedicated guys who are some of the best. Some I have known for quite some time and they know who they are. I will miss you all, all I can say is keep up the great job you are doing.
To my old mate Lushy: I know whatever the future holds for you in the 29th mate you'll do a great job.
If I haven't mentioned everyone it is because there is just too many great blokes in the unit I would be here all night..
Forever forward 29th! Catch you on the other side.
