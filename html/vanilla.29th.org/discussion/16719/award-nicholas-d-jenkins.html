Award (Nicholas D. Jenkins)

T4 Epler
February 2017 edited February 2017 in Promotions and Awards

T/5 Dethfield said:

Of all the SLT candidates I have seen in my short time in the 29th, PFC Jenkins has had one of the most interesting journies through it. Initially he seemed to be a somewhat questionable pick, and this view continued for some time. About 4 months ago his squad leadership seemingly disappeared without a trace and the already stuggling squad turned to PFC Jenkins for some semblance of leadership. To the absolute shock of everyone in AP3, PFC Jenkins did a complete 180 and did absolutely everything he could as an SLT candidate to keep the squad together and motivated. It was as if he was a completely different person, constantly working to keep the squad's morale up and keep them training and focused. By the end of 2016 everyone saw him as the true leadership in AP3S2 even if he did not hold such a position. This story may seem somewhat inappropriate but I feel it is important to illustrate the kind of path and achievment PFC Jenkins has followed in SLT. It truly was a sight to behold.

When I was made Squad Leader of AP3S2 one month ago, PFC Jenkins was made my official ASL. Today we have brought the squad back from a dark time to where it is now - a solid squad of soldiers who hold their own in drills, have excellent attendance and great morale. We have some new faces and they are quickly integrating themselves into the squad. While I like to think I had a hand in this, the truth is PFC Jenkins made it all possible by keeping the squad intact in the first place. At this point, anyone who feels PFC Jenkins is unfit to lead needs to be sent to me and straightened out. He truly deserves his NCO Professional Development Ribbon.
