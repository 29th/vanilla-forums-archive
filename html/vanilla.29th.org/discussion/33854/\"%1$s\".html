Promotion (Mark R. Furie)

T4 Ceglar [Ret.]
September 2019 in Promotions and Awards

TSgt. Dethfield said:

"When a little Rec. Furie entered the first BCT training platoon I ever taught, I did not expect to see him a year and a half later being one of the most capable NCOs in the company. After I witnessed him fall into our barbed wire pit and die, I certainly didn't expect him to be proving this capability over and over again over the course of his time as a Corporal. Like many others who enlisted during those early days of Easy Company, Furie did not know what to expect in the 29th any more than we did in the game of Squad. Yet, just as Easy Company found its footing and has flourished, so to did Furie in EP2. He quickly became recognizable as a bigger, bolder, better Grimes. Always willing to lend a hand in anything from combat assignments to leadership to staff work, Cpl. Furie may be soft-spoken but he has made a huge impact on the unit. His list of jobs is disgustingly long, as is his enthusiasm for leadership and although it always hasn't been smooth sailing, it has made him a better leader in the end. It seems only appropriate that we recognize his efforts with the coveted third chevron. Well done, Sgt. Furie. Grimes is probably crying in his sleep right about now."

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Mark R. Furie       025509269       EP2S1

I do promote him to Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 1st day of September 2019 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
