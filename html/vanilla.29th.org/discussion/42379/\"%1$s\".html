Promotion (Patrick K. Regan) 

T3 Grimes [Ret.]
December 2020 in Promotions and Awards

2Lt. Dahl said:

During the EP4 Scrim Prep PFC Regan took it upon himself to compile an entire tactical mock up of one of our possible approaches complete with maps and detailed explanations of sightlines that both us and our enemies would have and, in the end, we didn't even end up using that approach. Why am I telling you this you may ask, because I want to show you just how much effort PFC Regan puts into everything he does here. This also extends to one thing that he does here that may not always get the most recognition but, it is vital to everything we do here on a weekly basis, his clerking. PFC Regan has been consistently showing SSgt. Newton and I that he has not only mastered his duties as a clerk but, he is willing to go the extra mile to make sure that everything around EP4 continues to run smoothly. It is for that reason that SSgt. Newton and I don't think that the rank of PFC suits Regan very well anymore. So let me be the first to Congrats T/5 Regan! you've more than earned it keep up the good work.

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Patrick K. Regan       970203557      EP4S1

I do promote him to Technician, Fifth Grade in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 15th day of December 2020 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
