Tips For Playing a medic in squad

PFC Charlesworth
November 2020 in Public Discussion

The following tips may be more use on the public servers but I sure some can help you in the 29th drill & Scrim environments.

  1. Playing the medic
    • Stick close to your squad leader and slightly behind the fight. Tell the other members of the squad that you will not trek miles to heal them no matter how loud they scream! Firstly, this allows squad leader to do a lot of his duties, placing a rally etc and secondly it will hopefully let you manage the wounded quickly and get them back up and fighting. You can earn a lot of points as a medic.
    • If you are not doing medical duties, get your shovel out and help your squad build.
    • Get to the cap points with your squad.
    • If you are incapacitated and no chance of revival, ask your squad leader for permission to give up. The medic needs to get back into the fight as soon as possible.
    • If there are no medical duties and nothing to build or cap. Get your gun out but be prepared to put it away as soon as squad takes casualties.

  2. Healing tips
    • Staying alive is the most important, do not enter a “hot area” if you think you are unlikely to survive.
    • If casualties are making a lot of noise, either calling for medic in whatever language or talking loudly in chat. Tell them to “Shut up”. You need to be able to hear the enemy if they are near and there calls also alert the opposition that there may be more of your squad alive in the area.
    • When using field bandages, always try to be in prone position and position the casualty between you and any contact fire. (use him as a sandbag). Get in with dead and heal them. The number of times enemy have run past me thinking I dead is unbelievable.
    • As you treat the casualty, quietly tell him where the enemy are, and any safe locations nearby, (and if has to run or crawl). Or get him to smoke.
    • If you have a lot of casualties, concentrate on using field dressings. Only when everybody in reach is up, start using the medical kit.

  3. When using the medical kit
    • Always be a level lower than your patient. If he is standing, you crouch. If he is crouching, you lie prone. This allows him to secure the area for both of you. If he is prone again us him as a sandbag.
    • Always leave the patient a safe place to move into if you come under fire.

  4. General Tips
    • Only use smoke if the opposition know you are active. If they think you are dead, move quietly and do not throw smoke, talk or fire your weapon.
    • A medic should move fast and as quietly is possible. Leaping of blind walls is risky, but if the opposition are not alert you can get away with it.
    • If travelling by logistical truck, get to the truck early and sit in the f5 or f6 position. The opposite side to where you expect the enemy “might be”. So if travelling North and the opposition is expected from the East, Sit in West side of the truck (f5). The f5 and f6 get protection from cab, load area and other personnel. It is no guarantee of survival, but you have the best chance of survival of anybody in the vehicle.
    • If any vehicle traveling is hit, leave the vehicle as soon as you can and again take a prone position near the vehicle until you know what you intend to do.
    • Healing non-squad members. I heal everybody if your short of bandages don’t be afraid to ask a rifleman of another squad to rummage in his pack and resupply your medical equipment. Very often riflemen will offer it up without being asked.
