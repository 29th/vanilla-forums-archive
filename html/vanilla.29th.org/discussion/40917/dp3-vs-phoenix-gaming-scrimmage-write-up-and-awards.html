DP3 vs. Phoenix Gaming Scrimmage Write up and Awards

T5 Murray
October 2020 in Promotions and Awards



29TH INFANTRY DIVISION
116TH REGIMENT, 1ST BN
Dog COMPANY

Official Awards for Meritorious Service Before and During DP3's Deployment

September 27th, 2020


Second Lieutenant Matthew J. Brenholen

2Lt. Brenholen has participated in his second official scrimmage. For being a part of a two round scrimmage in which both rounds were a victory he will receive the World War II Victory Medal. In addition, for leading DP3 to victory in an official scrimmage 2Lt. Brenholen will receive the Legion of Merit.



Staff Sergeant Jacob A. Bruneau

SSgt. Bruneau has participated in his second official scrimmage. For being a part of a two round scrimmage in which both rounds were a victory he will receive the World War II Victory Medal.

I strongly believe SSgt. Bruneau has earned this medal because of his efficiency in being determined to find scrimmages with other units and successfully seeing them through to the end. He showed his capabilities in finding two units in quick succession and remained optimistic in finding more. He contributed to a huge moral boost to Dog company as a whole when setting out to find scrimmages so effectively. The reason I feel he went above and beyond his duties is while someone like him as Signal Corps Community Contact may just post info out there and hope to get some people looking to scrim he actively pursues not one but two units in a close time span and is an effective community contact from start to finish seeing that scrims go smoothly.



Sergeant Connor E. Lord

Sgt. Lord has participated in his second official scrimmage. For being a part of a two round scrimmage in which both rounds were a victory he will receive the World War II Victory Medal.

Sgt Lord was an effective CE during the scrim. He used his weapons both on defense and offense effectively. Netting the 3rd most kills in the scrim, five total between both rounds. He has proved to be effective and smart about his gameplay and communicated well with others. He ranged his thumper and got early hits as well as continual active artillery from lengthy range, his skills showed well and his leadership while getting mass kills just tops it off well. His prep in knowing ranges and high traffic areas where the CE weaponry would be effective took weeks to remember and become consistent in and that's why its so impressive that his practice netted him five kills.



Sergeant Aristidi L. Deb

Sgt. Deb has participated in his first official scrimmage and is therefore eligible for his Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. In addition, for being a part of a two round scrimmage in which both rounds were a victory he will receive the World War II Victory Medal.

Sgt. Deb methodically made callouts for all locations in the scrim in rigorous detail and provided immense planning instruction for the preparation of our attack and defense. This unified our Scrimmage members and immediately got us all on the same tactical mindset. These callouts were used heavily in the Scrim and drastically improved our gameplay from day one. Sgt. Deb created an entire Cu Chi 101 booklet for the scrim members to use. If not for his methodical determination and ambition our training and preparation would not have been as successful. The Sgt even took the time to go over all areas with everyone and was at every practice teaching people his hand made guide.

Technician, 5th Grade Dan Murray

T/5 Murray has participated in his first official scrimmage and is therefore eligible for his Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. In addition, for being a part of a two round scrimmage in which both rounds were a victory he will receive the World War II Victory Medal.

T/5 Murray had the play of the scrim. He held Yellow (East of Alpha) and cut down an entire enemy attack by himself. He took down a 8 man rush and put down 6 of them before the others fell back from the wall they were pushing through. If that push had gotten through they would have secured an intense tactical advantage over the other defenders in the area. His skills in CQC surpassed his regular play and while he didn't move around a lot, he held the one entrance into his building. He was able to survive and relay info to the rest of the team. His practice holding that area payed off as he knew the angles and where to smoke and Molotov.



Technician, 5th Grade Sasha Greeves

Private, First Class Luke M. Scovel

PFC Scovel has participated in his first official scrimmage and is therefore eligible for his Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. In addition, for being a part of a two round scrimmage in which both rounds were a victory he will receive the World War II Victory Medal.

PFC Scovel has always had an effect of charisma around him, but most people are aware of him because he is hard to kill. Scovel was one of my first choices for Machine Gunner during the scrim because I knew of his consistency to get results and to put people down. He has never failed to follow plans exactly and this showed in the scrim when he picked up 4-5 long to short range kills with his machine guns on both Northern and Southern teams. He held entire flanks on his own and was effective in calling out other targets he couldn't reach. His survival rate is extremely high and is someone I would want teaching upcoming Machine Gunner AIT holders.



Private, First Class Dean A. Gholson

PFC Gholson has participated in his first official scrimmage and is therefore eligible for his Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. In addition, for being a part of a two round scrimmage in which both rounds were a victory he will receive the World War II Victory Medal.

PFC Gholson was been practicing his area views for the last month, finding the exact high traffic areas where people move from spot to spot and the timing and lead and drop each shot would take. He brought down five guys, some at over 300 meters. He had to maneuver from area to area quickly as the battle progressed to hit different stages of enemy movement. The enemy moved quick and once they got setup in certain location he wouldn't be able to hit them, so he has to make sure to got them traveling. He also called out enemies in certain locations for our skirmish team which lead to even more success. He produced good results from his practice and is a model for upcoming snipers to learn from.
