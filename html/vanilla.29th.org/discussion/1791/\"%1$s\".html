Honorable Discharge (Moates, Jeff G.)

Cpt. Hill [Ret.]
February 2015 edited February 2015 in Discharges

HONORABLE DISCHARGE

FROM THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

This is to certify that


Jeff G. Moates    2Lt    CP1

was Honorably Discharged from the

UNITED STATES ARMY

on the 23st day of February 2015. This certificate is awarded

as a testimonial of Honest and Faithful Service





ARPC-OPM-O

DATE: 21 Jan 2015

MEMORANDUM FOR Commander,
U.S. Army 29th Infantry Division, ATTN: AHRC-OP-S, 1 Reserve Way
Fort Belvoir, VA 22060


SUBJECT: Unqualified Resignation


1.I hereby tender my unqualified resignation as an Enlisted Soldier in the
United States Army under the provisions of Chapter 6, Section III,
AR 135-175.

2.I understand that if my resignation is accepted I am entitled to an
honorable separation and will be furnished an Honorable Discharge
Certificate. I understand that I cannot resign if I am a statutory obligor.


Signature
Jeff G. Moates
Name (Type or Print)
2nd Lieutenant Platoon Leader Charlie Platoon One
Rank Position Platoon/Squad
From 2Lt. Moates:

As with everything in life, people and things change with time. It’s time for me to move on and focus my efforts on real-life endeavors. I’ve grown tremendously as an individual during my service in the 29th over the past 5 years and 5 months. I’ve met some of my best friends, broke out of my shell, and learned how to work with people from varying nationalities and backgrounds. It is with joyous reflection that I can honestly say that I’ve shared some of the best moments of my life with 29th members, whether in the heat of a match or at the crack of dawn in vent or TS.

The raw competitive teamwork and natural bond I experienced serving in and leading a squad and platoon in the 29th was unmatched. This is the only unit for me, thus you can be sure that at this point in my life I just need to take a step away from organized gaming and focus on life. In the words of my famous Governator, I’ll be back!
Nevertheless, as an ex-platoon clerk, ASL, SL, Platoon Sgt. and Platoon Leader who posted and wrote countless sappy AOCC’s and awards, I can’t help but torture you all one last time.

(In no particular order)

TSgt. Balzer – You were, are, and always will be an inspiration to me. You proved that leadership in a video game could be and was actual leadership. You raised a band of misfits who would have invaded hell and made it through for you. Yes we were cocky bastards, but we both know why. Your dedication, attention to detail, personality, and ability to adapt and make quick decisions led us to earn that right. I’ve learned a lot from you which I will carry onward in life. You are clearly a member of 29th who has put their all into it, strictly for the experience of those under you. Having risen above I can honestly say that that is one of the most respectful and difficult things someone can do. I speak for the entirety of CP1 and the 29th, thank you.

Sgt. Frank – This may be the hardest one of all. Yes we’ve grown distant with the rank silliness and my busyness but you are and always will be one of my closest friends. We grew up practically holding hands on the romantic beaches and fields of virtual Western Europe and they were some of the damn best times I’ve had in and out of a game. Then we entered SLT together, led a squad together, and won a deployment, campaign, and a BOTS together. We are both extremely competitive and everyone knows it. Blame the guy above. Yet at the end of the day everyone was better for it. We took the torch and bred a hell of a squad. Part of me actually can’t wait to move on, so that I can be myself around you again. I don’t know why it is, but it is. Thank you for always being there through the years and giving it your all with all of us.

SSgt. Bergstrom – We’ll call this one a late bloomer. For some reason we didn’t get to know each other for quite some time. I can now say we were stupid. Over the past couple years you have gone from the mysterious tall PFC to a great friend, an amazing leader, and someone who I better freaking meet one day. If someone would have said I would have led a platoon with you years back I would have said they were insane. Now, I can’t see it having gone any other way. We make a great team and if my circumstances were different, there’s no telling how far we could have gone. I do know one thing, with or without me you will push on and be an inspiration to everyone, just as TSgt. was to us. You have adapted extremely well to Arma and I bow and applaud you for that. So much so, that I forgive the ladder falls and rock ramming exploits during my OCS. It’s been a long time coming, but you’ve earned it you hipster poet. Press on Bergypoh.

CP1 (old and new) – If I tried to name every one of you that I wanted to, serving and discharged, I’d literally age a year in the process. You guys are truly amazing. You know who you are. You drove my motivation for years, made it not a game, but a life. You put a smile on my face every night I sat in this deteriorating chair and put on my worn headset. When I look at my steam profile and see over a thousand hours in DH, and hundreds in Arma 2 and 3, I realize just how much time I’ve spent with you guys. The laughs, the competition, the planning, the drills, the bullshitting, the reality checks, and the personal conversations all amount to one fact. This isn’t a clan, it’s a brotherhood. As cheesy as it sounds I do consider you my brothers. Thank you for giving me the opportunity to get to know each and every one of you. It’s been the upmost of pleasure and I can’t wait to finally meet some of you. Marshall and Ramos can’t hold that trophy for long, one of you has to steal it!

FSgt. Hill, Cpt. Morra, 2Lt. Turczyn, CP2, and more – You guys are true 29th veterans. We’ve shared amazing competition and amazing teamwork. I hold you guys in deep regard and will always cherish the times we’ve had together on and off the field. Tensions occasionally ran high in the past, as they naturally would in our situations, but boy was it not a hell of a lot of fun? When we swapped things around with Arma, I can thankfully say that I finally began to get to know you guys on a new level. You weren’t just extremely bad ass and deadly competition, but good guys, leaders, and just as dedicated. FSgt., you have put a tremendous effort into Arma 3 and it would have faded long ago without you and those other knuckleheads above. CP2, old and new, you guys are one hell of a force. Thank you for always being there.

Lighthouse Era – I learned so much serving with you guys. Chev, T-man, you were my rocks. We held that ship tight and transitioned from one game to three. You both put in a profound level of effort and dedication and I was happy to grow close to you both. We need a reunion. It wasn’t easy but I feel we pulled it off. DI’s, ADI’s, EL’s, Postal Secretaries, Steam community managers, and everyone else, you were just as big, if not a bigger part, in that success. It was your boots on the ground that inspired new cadets to work to join our ranks. You all deserve a glorious photo, say cheese!

Battalion HQ – All I want to say is that I deeply thank you for taking your time and running this unit without getting the chance to play. The less you are in game, the harder this becomes, and you guys have it hard. Yet you press on, and I deeply respect that. Thank you for putting all of this in place and giving me the opportunity to be a part of it for all of these years.
Reason for leaving
