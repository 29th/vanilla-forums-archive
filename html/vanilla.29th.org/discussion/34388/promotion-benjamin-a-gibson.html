Promotion (Benjamin A. Gibson)

T3 Grimes [Ret.]
October 2019 in Promotions and Awards

2Lt. Kelso said:

I've known the name Gibson since back in the Able days, never really knew him, but I saw him. Didn't get to really meet him until he became my ASL for EP3S1 back in May of 2018. He quickly impressed me, and grew immensely during that time. He immediately set himself upon helping with S1 and making it a excellent running squad. Unfortunately, our time was cut short in S1 but he returned to EP3 weeks later becoming a ASL under Sgt. Curran. He continued with performance and only grew from there, showing himself to be a top-notch ASL and was rewarded with becoming Squad Leader of EP3S4. He took the reigns of S4 along with Cpl. Hussey and shaped them into the excellent squad that they are today. In addition to his squad leadership, he has immersed himself in the world of staff jobs; reviving Veteran Virtues in the Civil Affairs office and reorganizing the Enlistment Liason's in Lighthouse. Time and time he has impressed, and I am extremely proud to promote him to the rank of Sergeant, congratulations Sergeant!

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Benjamin A. Gibson       145404349      EP3S3

I do promote him to Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 8th day of October 2019 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
