Promotion and Award (Patrik L. Ljung)

T4 Orhan
February 2017 edited February 2017 in Promotions and Awards

2Lt. Dashwood said:

PFC Ljung has done an absolutely outstanding job ever since he became ASL of BP1S3. He had been in SLT for a while before hand and had shown great promise in it, this made him a natural choice for leadership when the position became available. Time and time again Ljung has shown stalwart dedication to the 29th and has taken his position very seriously. He is an excellent compliment to Sgt. J. Corrions already effective style of leadership and the two of them together have become a driving force within the Platoon.

Ljung has also committed himself to the arduous task of map-making in DH, having already produced a workable and impressive first-map. His feats do not stop there, however, as PFC Ljung is also one of the companies few dedicated mortar-operators, this challenging and very hard-to-master AIT was no great difficulty to Ljung who very quickly received the sharpshooter badge for his skill.

All-in-all, Ljung has taken to every challenge laid before him and mastered many of the hardest skills in the unit. It is now time to add yet another string to his bow and award him for his achievements in leadership. Having proven himself in combat, on and off the field, consistently reaching and exceeding platoons expectations of him, I am delighted to award him, for completion of SLT, his Army NCO Professional Development Ribbon and a Promotion to the rank of Corporal! Congratulations Cpl. Ljung!

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Patrik L. Ljung      967824699     BP1S3

I do promote him to Corporal in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 16th day of February 2017.

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
