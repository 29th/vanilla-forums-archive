Promotion (Christian D. Robles)

T3 Grimes [Ret.]
July 2020 edited July 2020 in Promotions and Awards

FSgt. Dethfield said:

When Easy company began to hit high numbers, it became clear that the workload was increasing quickly for platoon and company HQs. More members meant more people to keep track of, more awards and badges to watch for, more disciplinary matters to attend to and so on. With EBOP looming, we realized that we will need a third pair of hands this summer. SSgt. Robles was the obvious choice, seeing as he was not only running EBOP anyway but he has been with Easy company since the beginning and has been one of the most influential NCOs throughout the company's lifetime. He has so far proven not only to be an excellent member of company HQ but is also a great example of how useful and impactful the role of company sergeant can be and I now believe having a third man in company headquarters is a very good idea in general. Because of his amazing contributions to the company and serving as an excellent example of what a Company Sergeant can do, we are proud to promote him to the rank of Technical Sergeant.

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Christian D. Robles       109051248      Easy Company HQ

I do promote him to Technical Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 26th day of July 2020 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
