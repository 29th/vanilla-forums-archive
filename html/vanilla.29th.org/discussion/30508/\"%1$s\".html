Award (Marcus V. Zim)

T5 Kiostri [Ret.]
March 2019 in Promotions and Awards

@“2Lt. Nelson” said:

Setting:
Friday Night Fight, 22 February 2019 - Opfor (29th ID +) assaulting Bluefor (2nd MRB +) held compound in the SW corner of the NW airfield, Chernarus.

>

Objective:
Hack 2x terminals (2 min timers on each)

>

Charlie Company, 29th Infantry Division (Designated Bravo Squad - 2x Infantry fire-teams + 2Lt. Nelson as SL), was tasked to kick off the Opfor assault on the SE side of the Airfield after our team's GAZ (manned by 2x 29thers) and Tank did some work from afar. We were concerned that we'd have to cross open ground on the Tarmac, but there was a substantial amount of construction work between us and the objective on the south side of the airfield that gave us a good approach. PFC Brady in the GAZ managed to ferry us across the Tarmac to gain a foothold in the construction area, successfully getting both fire-teams into position unseen. We then proceeded to slowly clear each building till we were ready to cross the next gap to the H-Barriers forming eastern perimeter of the enemy position. While doing so, T/5 Zim neutralized two enemy contacts in the stairwell of the north objective tower, and the H-Barrier tower, greatly contributing to securing the area.

>

The south-west assault element (Alpha) was bogged down in combat, so we felt it was time to make our push. The objectives were split north and south within the two sections of enemy base, so we focused on the south side first. With T/5 Zim in the local high-ground of a tall industrial building, we proceeded to frag and smoke over the walls and pushed in with extreme prejudice. 2Lt. Nelson was downed by a frag-barrage survivor on the south side, but was promptly neutralized by PFC Scholand. Once the south side was clear and secured, PFC Robertson regrouped the men to move onto the north objective. Once clear, T/5 Zim descended from his position and joined in the ground-work.

>

It is worth noting that at this point, our Tank - which had situated itself on the tarmac about 200m north - had been neutralized and possibly captured by the 2nd MRB. As well, The entire southern assault element (Alpha ~8 men from ) was combat-ineffective and off the net, so for all we knew it was just us left to follow through with the objective. With that in mind and the south section cleared, T/5 Zim was sent around to flank the eastern perimeter of the northern base, clearing each position with grenades and rifle fire while the rest of the squad pushed in from the south; the enemy had no escape. Once the buildings were deemed secure, T/5 Zim lead the charge up a set of two large military cargo towers in the center, which held the last terminal. After a brutal CQB fight under withering MG fire from the tank (unknown if friend or foe), T/5 Zim secured and hacked the terminal, triggering a 2 minute timer.

>

A small gaggle of Bluefor appeared from the south, ripping through our ground-security team and pushing up the south tower (our guys held the north). Hearing footsteps clanking on metal floors to his south, T/5 Zim prepared for the inevitable. The first one dashed across the rooftop doorway, catching a bullet from T/5 Zim's AK-74. Sensing danger, T/5 Zim re-positioned downstairs, rallying PFC Scholand to cover the stairs with him. The second man then careened down the stairway, landing directly in front of T/5 Zim's muzzle. As the timer ticked through it's final seconds, T/5 Zim called for his comrades to clear the area in anticipation for an explosion. Thankfully, he lived through the duration and, wiping the sweat from his brow, he descended the tower into the glory of victory.

>

The level of self-sacrifice and commitment to protecting his squad shows great initiative, all the while maintaining focus on the objective at hand. Without the contributions of T/5 Zim, we quite possibly would have been whittled down to a small handful of men, resulting in low morale and a disorganized push. His performance should be aspired to by all.
