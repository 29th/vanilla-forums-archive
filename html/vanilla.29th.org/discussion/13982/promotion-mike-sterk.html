Promotion (Mike Sterk)

T3 Nielsen [Ret.]
September 2016 edited September 2016 in Promotions and Awards

2Lt. Derbyshire said:

Corporal Sterk is an exemplary leader both on and off the battlefield who never fails to get the job done and always impresses.

When BP2 HQ said that we wanted squads to improve on fire and maneuver, Corporal Sterk took S1 by the horns and he steered them, he found their weaknesses and he build on them, he developed fire and maneuver with his ASL to suit the squad and between them they made S1 into a hard fighting force.

When BP2 HQ said that the platoon as a whole needed to improve on communication, Corporal Sterk was the first leader within BP2 to impress HQ by implementing communication techniques within his squad which meant he could always communicate with HQ to the standard we required and more.

It is Corporal Sterk, in BP2S1, that has really brought the squad on leaps and bounds, he constantly spots where the squad is failing and he makes them stronger on those points.

As a Platoon Leader, I know that I can fully rely on Corporal Sterk and his opinion, he has experience and knowledge which he uses to his advantage and he always comes out with the performance I require. He is an example to other leaders and an example to his men, he is what I expect the rest of my platoon to aspire to.

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Mike Sterk       983575019       BP2S1

I do promote him to SERGEANT in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 7th day of September 2016 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
