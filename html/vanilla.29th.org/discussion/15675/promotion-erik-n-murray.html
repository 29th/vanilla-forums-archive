Promotion (Erik N. Murray)

T4 Burg
November 2016 edited November 2016 in Promotions and Awards

SSgt. Meyberg said:

Cpl. Murray where do I start, 1 year and 7months ago Cpl. Murray joined the 29th he first started out in Able but luckily for our Cpl. He got his brains back after a little more than 4 months at the moment he decided to transfer to Charlie. He earned his ticks and started to make quite a name for himself.

Now over a year later he is the Squad leader of Squad 1 and what a squad leader he is. I think many squad leaders can confirm that running a squad of 10 people can be a pain in the ass sometimes but Cpl. Murray doesn’t have a squad of 10 or 12. No, his squad has 14 people! Which is a lot, But Cpl. Murray has done a very good job at keeping those 14 soldiers in line.
Cpl. Murray is also the one I go to when I need something to get done. When I leave for a trip to Sweden for example. Cpl. Murray is the one filling in the blanks.
It’s a pleasure to promote Cpl. Murray to the rank of Sgt.

Congratulation Sgt. Murray

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Erik N. Murray      050461699     CP2S1

I do promote him to Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 27th day of November 2016 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
