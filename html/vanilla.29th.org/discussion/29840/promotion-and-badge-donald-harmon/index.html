Promotion and Badge (Donald Harmon)

T4 Ceglar [Ret.]
January 2019 edited January 2019 in Promotions and Awards

SSgt. Dethfield said:

PFC Harmon has found himself in rather historical position. Not only was the the first platoon sniper to be assigned to Easy company but was also tasked with actually establishing a lot of what a platoon sniper can and cannot do in the game of Squad. When we began to consider the issues surrounding the role in Easy company, it became clear that it would not necessarily be the massive killing machine it had been in previously played games in the 29th. Communication, tracking enemy movement, locating priority targets... those would be the roles that the platoon sniper would truly be valuable for. Killing loads of enemies, while still a required skill for our sniper, would be clearly a secondary role.

When PFC Harmon first expressed interest, I suspect he hesitated slightly when I informed of what would be required for the role in my platoon. I did not want someone who just sat in silence trying to climb the scoreboard. I wanted someone who would be as involved in the platoon as any Squad Leader, someone who would truly be the expert in game mechanics and someone who would actively help teach our riflemen how to git gud. He eventually decided that he would learn what he needed to accomplish his goal, and I stand here today announcing that he has. PFC Harmon is a prime example of an excellent platoon sniper and someone with the drive to put in the work to achieve his goals here. He has rightfully earned his Sniper Marksman Badge and a promotion to Technician, 5th Grade.

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Donald Harmon       120426053       EP2S1

I do promote him to Technician, 5th Grade in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 21st day of January 2019 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
