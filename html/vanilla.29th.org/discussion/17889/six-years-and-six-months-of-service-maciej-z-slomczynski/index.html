Six Years and Six Months of Service (Maciej Z. Slomczynski )

Cpt. Patty
April 2017 edited April 2017 in Promotions and Awards

I am tired of singing praise for this seemingly perfect, multi-talented T/3 and his wizardly clerking skills. Filling both the Baker Company and Battalion clerk roles AND the Chief Technician role of the Website (and the caretaker of it too).

He is Constantly attentive, nothing too small to slip his attention, well liked by all who have the pleasure of talking to him. Indeed he is like a pilot master, helping the Captains and Admirals of the 29th fleet steer their weather worn ships into safe ports and clear blue seas.

However I can reveal some troubling things about the apparently un-slanderable T/3 Slomczynski...

Like a salty sailor, his attitude can sometimes be less than earnestly keen and fervent, as one would expect of other clerks. Instead he can sometimes come across as obstinate, with the attitude of someone used to being right. Indeed he could even be described as cantankerous at times, with little patience for lesser beings with less knowledge of the disciplines of clerking and technicianing.

Secondly, his constant attentiveness to his work and tireless exertion to his duties could be seen to some as over the top. Distressing his colleges into thinking they themselves are not doing as much work and setting the bar to a vexingly high standard, impossible to achieve by others. Many people find this aptitude of his with deep suspicion, such apple-polishing behaviour must indicate some kind of sinister ulterior motive...

Lastly his rapid advancement into the lofty heights of Technician 3rd Grade has lifted him from his former position of Machine-Gunner, robbing us of a talented and promising soldier. Instead he now lurks with the other HQ during drills. One can only question the motives of someone who thinks they are above crawling in the mud, forgoing lugging a heavy piece of equipment around with his fellow comrades, to instead laze in the rear echelons during combat.

However with much reluctance we must accept T/3 Slomczynski despite his numerous flaws. Even though he has his (many) shortcomings he is still proven to be a (somewhat) useful 29th'er, and for that we must (grudgingly) congratulate him for his six years and six months of service. I hereby (hesitantly) award T/3 Slomczynski his 13th Army of Occupation Medal.

Congratulations I guess.
