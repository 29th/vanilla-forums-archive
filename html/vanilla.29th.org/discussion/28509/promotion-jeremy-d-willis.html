Promotion (Jeremy D. Willis) 

T4 Garrett
October 2018 in Promotions and Awards

SSgt. Dethfield said:

Corporal Willis has had a wild ride since the expansion last year. Being an original member of EP2S1 as a T/5 and quickly moving up to the ASL spot under Sgt. Boelter, Willis continued to improve his leadership skills while also learning the game that we were all new to. Upon becoming the squad leader of EP2S1, he inherited a small squad with a solid core of members but was struggling in other areas. Fortunately, he was willing to learn from the more experienced sergeants around him and all of this effort has paid off. EP2S1 now currently has the best squad attendance in Easy company, is home to 3 excellent SLT candidates, and have become well known for their excellent use of the various weapons and assets of the game (something others continue to struggle with). It is safe to say that Cpl. Willis is one of the primary factors for this huge, hasty jump in squad effectiveness and in recognition of his efforts I am proud to give him his third chevron. Awesome job, Sgt. Willis!

CERTIFICATION OF PROMOTION

TO ALL WHO SHALL SEE THESE PRESENTS, GREETING:

Know Ye, that reposing special trust and
confidence in the fidelity and abilities of

Jeremy D. Willis       987752132      EP2S1

I do promote him to Sergeant in the

UNITED STATES ARMY

to rank as such from the 1st day of October 2018 .

You are charged to discharge carefully and diligently the duties of the grade to which promoted and to uphold the traditions and standards of the Army.

Effective with this promotion you are charged to execute diligently your special skills with a high degree of technical proficiency and to maintain standards of performance, moral courage and dedication to the Army which will serve as outstanding examples to your fellow soldiers. You are charged to observe and follow the orders and directions given by superiors acting according to the law, articles and rules governing the discipline of the Army. Your unfailing trust in superiors and loyalty to your peers will significantly contribute to the readiness and honor of the United States Army.
