Sergeant Dan Vos

Sgt. Vos has participated in his first official scrimmage and will receive the Combat Infantry Badge, 1st Award. For winning the defense round during an official scrimmage he will receive the Defense Meritorious Service Medal.

Sgt. Vos was the man who held the door and did more than could have been asked from him when the enemy came knocking. After the team had been engaged in delaying tactics for most of the round, the enemy was forced to push into town on the north western flank. Prior to this, they had been spotted moving and Sgt. Vos had put himself in position to intercept the enemy as they were making their approach, and as friendly forces were still redeploying in the town to reinforce the flank. His call was the first call that confirmed the enemy were breaching the town and he soon found himself surrounded by enemies on all flanks. Fear did not grip him in this moment and he began to hammer the enemy forces with grenandes and rifle fire, all while communicating the enemy positions through the radio networks. With his intel, 2nd platoon were pulled back and deployed onto the southern flank of the enemy and despite all odds Sgt. Vos kept transmitting. Through a mixture of right time and right place, and nerves of steel, Sgt. Vos was able to down or injure 18 enemy members equating to 33% of the enemy forces. Without his stalwart and steadfast defence, the attackers would have maintained their momentum and swarmed the western cache but he took the wind from their sails until his death. Congratulations on receiving your Bronze Star!"

    29TH INFANTRY DIVISION
    116TH REGIMENT, 1ST BN
    CHARLIE COMPANY

    OPERATION LUMBERJACK 29th vs TAU/FNF Coalition After Action Report



    SITUATION

    The 29th Infantry Division, Charlie Company is deploying to counter the threat from the Tau Empire and its coalition of FNF fighters. Having accepted a challenge from the 29th, the Tau Coalition is looking for a 60v60 fight across three rounds, attack/defense/neutral tie-breaker.
    This is a CP2 deployment with full reserve support from CP1.

    DATE/TIME
    30 January 2020, 1600 EST

    COMMAND
    2Lt. Stray, Company Commander
    2lLt. Drake, Company XO
    FSgt. Balzer, Company Sergeant
    SSgt. Vandewalle, First Platoon Leader
    SSgt. Halls, Second Platoon Leader

    Round 1 Defense (Ruha)

    ![](upload://uoDWcnXV2ILXDxIHp4dK90Xnbdt.jpeg)

    On January 30, 2021, the 29th Infantry Division deployed to Ruha to defend two weapons caches from an imminent attack by TAU/FNF coalition Forces.
    The Coalition Forces made a 3-pronged attack on the town of Dormte. In the south, a motorized team deployed to distract the 29th forces from the main assault in the North and East. 29th Second Platoon was able to spot this movement very early on, and repelled the attack with ease. The threat disengaged crippled from the battle, disorganised due to the heavy resistance they encountered from the 29th ID Forces, stationed south of the objective.
    The Coalition then proceeded the fully focus their attack from the North and East, but again they stumbled on the heavy resistance of the 29th ID Defenders. Each attack was turned down and repelled by the defenders.
    In a desperate last attempt to overrun the 29th defenders, the TAU/FNF Coalition gathered their forces out of view of the defenders to make a daring call. They mounted up in their motorized vehicles and drove in full force through the 29th defense line straight into the town of Dormte.
    Without hesistation, the 29th ID defending elements within the town, relied on their training to remain calm and decisive. Thanks to the excellent skill of the defenders and their teamwork, they were more then capable of turning the tides and repel the desperate attack of the Coalition. Both objectives remained intact.

    Round 2 Attack (Rosche)

    ![](upload://qOUw9gjgtyqntpShuZBydfbSQ4t.jpeg)

    On January 30, 2021, the 29th Infantry Division deployed to Rosche to attack and destroy two weapons caches defended by the TAU/FNF Coalition.
    The 29th Infantry Division made a daring move and stayed out of sight and contact off the enemy forces, giving them a guess where the attacking forces were.
    Only after sending out scouts, the movement of the 29th Infantry Division was spotted moments before they main assault started.
    Thanks to the stealth approach, the Coalition was caught of guard and disorganised. The 29th pushed from the North and the West into the town of Kaaranimikko. However the Coalition recovered from being disorganised in a quickly manner and the 29th ran into stiff resistance on both flanks. The element of surprise was gone, slowing down the attack. The Coalition swiftly moved into position and started a successful counterattack in the North, removing the threat of the 29th. Both objectives remained intact.

    Round 3 Tie-breaker Attack - Attack (Ruha)

    On January 30, 2021, the 29th Infantry Division deployed to Altis to gain control of the Kavala Castle, while fighting of the enemy resistance in said town.
    The 29th Infantry Division placed one platoon in the Town of Kavala itself, trying to secure our entrance to the Castle, while giving Second Platoon the order to flank the Kavala Castle from the North.
    Minutes after entering the town, First Platoon ran into heavy enemy resistance, and got pinned in a major urban close quarters fight with the Coalition. It was eventually the Coalition that took the upper hand in this fierce battle, and thus cutting of the supply lines to flanking elements of Second Platoon.
    While Second Platoon stayed out of the fights and moved around the Castle in a stealthy manner, they encountered difficulties climbing the rocks and high walls. When they reached their breaching point, the Coalition already set up defensive positions within the Castle, making it possible to defend the area from any attacking elements.
    The Coalition was able to maintain control over Kavala Castle, and eliminate the attacking forces.
