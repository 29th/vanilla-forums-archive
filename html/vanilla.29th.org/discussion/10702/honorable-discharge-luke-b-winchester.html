Honorable Discharge (Luke B. Winchester)

T3 Lauritsen
March 2016 edited March 2016 in Discharges


HONORABLE DISCHARGE

FROM THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA

This is to certify that

Luke B. Winchester    PFC    AP2S1

was Honorably Discharged from the

UNITED STATES ARMY

on the 29th day of MARCH 2016. This certificate is awarded

as a testimonial of Honest and Faithful Service





ARPC-OPM-O

DATE: 29 MAR 2016

MEMORANDUM FOR Commander,

U.S. Army 29th Infantry Division, ATTN: AHRC-OP-S, 1 Reserve Way

Fort Belvoir, VA 22060


SUBJECT: Unqualified Resignation


1.I hereby tender my unqualified resignation as an Enlisted Soldier in the

United States Army under the provisions of Chapter 6, Section III,

AR 135-175.

2.I understand that if my resignation is accepted I am entitled to an

honorable separation and will be furnished an Honorable Discharge

Certificate. I understand that I cannot resign if I am a statutory obligor.


Luke B. Winchester
Signature
Luke B. Winchester
Name (Type or Print)
PFC Squad Leader Able Company, Second Platoon, First Squad
Rank Position Platoon/Squad

Hello friends, I've decided at this point that it'd be in my best interest to request a discharge from the 29th. I've been mulling over the decision of whether or not to continue with the unit for a long time now, and I've started to reach the point where my time with the unit has worked its way into every aspect of my life.
I've reached the point where I've begun cancelling time with friends and my girlfriend of seven years to ensure that I'm available for drills - I've begun leaving my campus early when I should be working in order to ensure that I can make it home to prep for drills. I've gotten so used to putting other things to the wayside for the 29th that I'm no longer having fun with my commitment.
Free time is a rare thing for me - I'm currently working two jobs with a full time college schedule. Right now, I need to do what's best for me, and take some time to evaluate what's really important.
In my time as an online gamer, I can honestly say I have never experienced the same sense of community that the 29th Infantry Division has given me. In my year with the unit I've made some great friends from across the world, and I hope that with my departure I'll still be able to maintain a close connection with all of you. While I am leaving the 29th, I'd be happy to keep in touch. You can visit my actual steam account here.
It's been a pleasure serving with all of you, and I hope to continue playing together in the future.
Ever forward,
PFC Luke B. Winchester.
